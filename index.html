Case Study 3

Group Projects 2: All dogs Matter

Executive Summary: Changing lives for London's Dogs

Role & Responsibilities + Team Size: This is a group project and 3 people in the group, to develop a non-profit website redesign and make it responsive on desktop and mobile together.

Tools Used: Figma, Miro, google slide, google drive, invision, Zoom

Timeline & Scope: 3 weeks

Poject Description

Improve All Dogs Matter website UI to drive donations and support, increasing capacity to rescue and care for dogs in need.

Project Overview

To enhance the Ul of the All Dogs Matter's website, creating a visually appealing and user-friendly design that highlights donation opportunities and provides clear and innovative ways for visitors to support the cause. The aim is to increase website engagement and visitor donations leading to an increased capacity for rescuing and caring for dogs in need.

The Problem

Based on research we found our platform lacks the visual engagement and user-friendliness needed to be improved effectively to inspire users to make a donation. This hinders the donation experience and poses a risk of reduced support for dog-related causes.

The solutions

My organisation at All Dogs Matter is updating our website to create an immersive and engaging experience for dog lovers. With a playful paw shape button, interactive sliders, and animated pop-ups expressing gratitude for donations, we help our customers make a meaningful impact. Our enhanced design reinforces our commitment to rescuing and caring for dogs, earning trust through professionalism and dedication.

Phase 1: User research

During our research, we thoroughly analysed the heuristics of five competitors, paying close attention to key features that impact user experience. This involved evaluating elements such as the visibility and engagement of the donate button, user-friendly processes, the use of carousel images to captivate users, clickable links in the footer, and an intuitive drop-down menu for streamlined navigation. We also conducted focus group testing and surveys. Our main objective was to assess the website's aesthetics and usability. Although users didn't encounter significant issues during navigation, they expressed a strong desire for enhanced transparency regarding how their donations would be utilized and the resulting impact. It's worth noting that some users raised concerns about trustworthiness, which had a direct influence on their motivation to make donations.

Phase 3: Ideation

Using the "I like, I wish, what it" method and categorising them, we voted on a few of our favourite features, these were the top few like, 1. Clickable iconography and Chatbot puppy. 2.Clickable links in the footer. 3.Animal shaped buttons, interactive sliders and carousels. 4. Animated pop ups. 5.Contact shortcut Information hidden until hover over it.

Phase 4: Prototyping Low to High

A few comments we took in for changes were text size and donation design being too playful on our Low to Mid-fi prototypes. After a group discussion we decides to update the typeface in our navigation bar to enhance readability and create contrast to the rest of the page. And we also made adjustments by changing the body text for a friendlier yet more serious tone and made simple changes to the box.

Phase 5: User Testing and Outcomes

We started with a usability test plan, our primary objective is to assess the aesthetic usability of the website and the secondary objective is to learn about user browsing behaviour. Our target users are individuals in their mid 20s to early 30s who have experience with technology. We used Maze to do the usability testing and generated the results.
